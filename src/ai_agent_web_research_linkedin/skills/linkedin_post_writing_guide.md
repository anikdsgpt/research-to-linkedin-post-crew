# LinkedIn Post Writing Skill Guide

## Purpose
This skill guide ensures LinkedIn posts are professionally crafted, engaging, and optimized for maximum impact with a professional audience.

## Core Principles

### 1. Structure & Flow
- **Hook (First 1-2 lines)**: Capture attention immediately with a bold statement, surprising fact, or question
- **Body (3-6 paragraphs)**: Develop the narrative with clear insights and evidence
- **Conclusion (1-2 lines)**: End with actionable takeaway or thought-provoking statement
- **Call-to-Action (Optional)**: Subtle invitation to engage (question, invite thoughts, or share experience)

### 2. Content Quality Standards
- **Credibility**: Every claim should be backed by research, data, or expert insight
- **Clarity**: Use simple language; avoid jargon unless industry-specific and explained
- **Brevity**: Each paragraph should be 2-3 sentences maximum
- **Relevance**: Every sentence must serve the post's main message

### 3. Tone & Voice
- **Professional yet Conversational**: Write like speaking to a peer, not lecturing
- **Confident**: Demonstrate authority through evidence and structure
- **Inclusive**: Use "we" and "our" to create connection
- **Authentic**: Avoid buzzwords; use genuine, specific language

### 4. Formatting Best Practices
- **Line Breaks**: Use white space between paragraphs for readability
- **Bullet Points**: Use sparingly (3-5 maximum) for key takeaways or trends
- **Bold/Emphasis**: Highlight key concepts (3-5 instances per post)
- **Hashtags**: Include 3-5 relevant hashtags at the end, on separate line
- **Avoid**: All-caps, excessive punctuation, emojis (unless very strategic)

### 5. LinkedIn-Specific Tactics
- **Lead with Insight**: Start with a trend, statistic, or observation that resonates with target audience
- **Use Numbers**: Posts with statistics perform 3-5x better
- **Tell a Story**: Frame insights within a narrative or example
- **Ask Questions**: Posts that include questions get 2x more engagement
- **Provide Value**: Readers should walk away with at least one actionable idea
- **Mention Challenges**: Acknowledge industry pain points; audiences relate to problems before solutions

### 6. Post Length Guidance
- **Optimal**: 100-300 words (easily readable, shares well)
- **Extended**: 300-500 words (for deep dives on complex topics)
- **Avoid**: Posts under 50 words (insufficient depth) or over 800 words (kills engagement)

### 7. Validation Checklist Before Publishing
- [ ] Does the first sentence hook the reader?
- [ ] Is every claim supported by research or data?
- [ ] Are key takeaways bold and visible?
- [ ] Is the tone professional yet conversational?
- [ ] Does it end with a clear, actionable insight?
- [ ] Are there no grammatical or spelling errors?
- [ ] Would someone in the target industry find this valuable?
- [ ] Is the formatting clean and readable (short lines, white space)?
- [ ] Are relevant hashtags included?

### 8. What to Avoid
- ❌ Generic corporate speak ("synergize," "circle back," "low-hanging fruit")
- ❌ Unsubstantiated claims without sources or data
- ❌ Posts longer than 500 words without visual breaks
- ❌ Excessive self-promotion (should be 80% value, 20% subtle promotion)
- ❌ Spelling, grammar, or punctuation errors
- ❌ Clickbait or misleading headlines
- ❌ Engagement-bait questions ("Like if you agree")
- ❌ Reposting others' content without original perspective

### 9. Research Integration Best Practices
When using research from upstream tasks:
- **Synthesize, Don't Repeat**: Combine insights from multiple sources into cohesive narrative
- **Add Perspective**: Explain *why* the research matters to the reader
- **Use Specifics**: Reference trends, stats, and examples from research, not generic statements
- **Cross-Validate**: If research contradicts, acknowledge nuance or prioritize more credible source
- **Contextualize**: Frame research within broader industry conversations

### 10. Engagement Optimization
- **Open Rate Killers**: Avoid starting with "I," "We," "If you," or marketing phrases
- **Engagement Drivers**: Start with "Interesting," "New research shows," "Most people don't realize," "Here's what nobody talks about"
- **Close with Questions**: Genuine questions drive 2-3x more comments
- **Admit Complexity**: "This isn't simple" or "Here's the catch" create credibility
- **Use Data Patterns**: "7 out of 10" or "65% of" feels more authentic than "most"

### 11. Template for High-Performing Posts
```
[HOOK: Bold observation or question]

[CONTEXT: Why this matters now]

[KEY INSIGHT 1]: Details and supporting evidence
[KEY INSIGHT 2]: Details and supporting evidence  
[KEY INSIGHT 3]: Details and supporting evidence

[CONCLUSION: Actionable takeaway or forward-looking statement]

[SOFT CTA: Question or invitation to comment]

#hashtag1 #hashtag2 #hashtag3
```

### 12. Industry-Specific Considerations
- **B2B Posts**: Focus on ROI, efficiency gains, risk mitigation
- **Professional Development**: Practical skills, career tips, industry trends
- **Tech & Innovation**: Keep technical, but explain real-world applications
- **Sales & Business Development**: Show outcomes, provide predictable patterns, enable action

### 13. Final Quality Gates
- **Readability**: Can this be skimmed in under 30 seconds?
- **Retention**: Will the reader remember one key takeaway?
- **Relevance**: Is this valuable to someone in the target industry?
- **Shareability**: Would a professional want to share this?
- **Authenticity**: Does this sound like a real person, not an algorithm?

---

**Remember**: The goal is to add value to someone's professional day, build trust through insights, and position yourself or your organization as knowledgeable and reliable.
